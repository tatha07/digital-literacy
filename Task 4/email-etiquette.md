# Social Media Do's and Don'ts

## ✅ Do's
- Use professional language and tone
- Think before posting
- Keep your profiles updated
- Respect others' opinions
- Protect your privacy settings

## ❌ Don'ts
- Share personal or sensitive information
- Post offensive or inappropriate content
- Spread misinformation
- Engage in online arguments
- Use unprofessional language

Poor digital communication can lead to serious misunderstandings and missed opportunities. For example, a student once sent an email to a professor without a proper subject line or greeting and used informal language. The message was unclear and appeared unprofessional, which resulted in the professor ignoring the email. As a result, the student missed an important deadline.

This situation could have been avoided by following proper email etiquette, such as using a clear subject line, addressing the recipient respectfully, and writing a well-structured message. Maintaining a professional tone and checking for errors before sending the email is also important. Effective digital communication helps build a positive impression and ensures that messages are understood clearly. This is especially important for students when interacting with professors or applying for internships.
