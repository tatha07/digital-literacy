Part A – Case Study (Phishing)

# Cybercrime Case Study: Phishing Attack

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted entity. This is usually done through fake emails, messages, or websites that look genuine.

A typical phishing attack happens in several steps. First, the attacker sends a message that appears to be from a bank, company, or service provider. The message often creates urgency, such as warning about account suspension or offering a reward. Next, the victim is asked to click a link, which redirects them to a fake website that looks real. The victim then enters their login details or financial information. Once the attacker gets this information, they use it to access accounts or steal money.

Students and young adults are common targets because they frequently use online platforms and may not always verify links carefully. The consequences of phishing can be serious, including financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to personal accounts.

This type of cybercrime highlights the importance of awareness, careful verification of links, and safe online practices to avoid becoming a victim.


Part B – Prevention Checklist

# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist

## 🔒 General Safety Tips
- Always verify the sender before clicking on any link.
- Do not share personal information like passwords or OTPs.
- Use strong and unique passwords for different accounts.
- Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) wherever possible.
- Avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive activities.
- Keep your software and apps updated regularly.

## 💳 UPI & Financial Safety
- Never share your UPI PIN with anyone.
- Do not approve unknown payment requests.
- Double-check UPI IDs before sending money.
- Avoid scanning unknown QR codes.

## 🚨 Reporting Cybercrime
- Report incidents at: https://cybercrime.gov.in
- Helpline Number: 1930
